Aman Dhattarwal


Aman Dhattarwal is an Indian educator, entrepreneur, and digital content creator known for democratizing education through online platforms. He founded Apni Kaksha and Apna College, popular YouTube-based ventures that provide free academic and career guidance to millions of students across India and beyond.

Key Facts :
  • Born: March 4, 1997, Rajasthan, India
  • Education: B.E. in Information Technology, Netaji Subhas Institute of Technology, Delhi
  • Spouse: Shradha Khapra (m. 2023)
  • Notable Roles: Founder of Apni Kaksha and Apna College; Former top educator at Unacademy
  • YouTube Reach: 10 million+ combined subscribers (2024)

Early Life and Education

Raised in Delhi, Dhattarwal excelled academically, scoring 95 percent in his CBSE Class 12 exams. He attended Delhi Public School (Vasant Kunj and R.K. Puram) before completing an engineering degree from NSIT. Initially reserved, he overcame stage fear through active participation in public events, an experience that later shaped his teaching persona.


Career and Online Ventures

Dhattarwal launched his self-titled YouTube channel in 2015, offering free lessons on board-exam preparation and career planning. The channel’s success led to the creation of Apni Kaksha (focused on Class 12 and competitive exams) and Apna College (focused on coding and placements). His work at Unacademy made him one of the platform’s top-rated IIT JEE instructors. He later established the Hustlers Bay channel to share motivational stories.


Impact and Recognition

Dhattarwal has delivered over 20 TEDx talks and guest lectures at institutions such as IIT Bombay, BITS Pilani, and Delhi University. His mission to “teach 5 crore students” underscores a broader goal of making high-quality, holistic education freely accessible. He also coordinated with Jaipur Foot USA, a major NGO supporting people with disabilities.


Personal Life and Public Image

Aman Dhattarwal married fellow educator and content creator Shradha Khapra in 2023. Known online as “Aman Bhaiya,” he combines academics with personal development advice, inspiring millions of young learners. His net worth is estimated between ₹5–8 crore (≈ US $1 million – $1.2 million) as of 2024.
