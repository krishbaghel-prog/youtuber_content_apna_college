Shradha Khapra


Shradha Khapra is an Indian educator, entrepreneur, and content creator best known as the co-founder of Apna College, a leading online learning platform focused on coding and career preparation. Popularly called “Microsoft wali Didi”, she is celebrated for making technical education accessible to students across India through relatable, practical teaching.

Key facts
  • Born: August 1, 2000 (Haryana, India)
  • Education: B.Tech in Computer Engineering, Netaji Subhas Institute of Technology (Delhi)
  • Former roles: Software Engineer at Microsoft; intern at DRDO and Google SPS
  • Known for: Co-founding Apna College with educator Aman Dhattarwal
  • Net worth (2025 est.): ₹12 – 15 crore INR

Early life and education

Raised in Nathurpur, Haryana, Khapra was the first woman from her village to pursue engineering. Excelling academically, she scored 10 CGPA in Class 10 and 94.4% in Class 12 from Jain Bharati Mrigavati Vidyalaya before earning her B.Tech degree from NSIT Delhi. Her technical curiosity and perseverance earned her internships at DRDO and Google SPS, followed by a role at Microsoft’s Azure team where she worked on cloud and system design projects.


Career and Apna College

In 2020, Khapra left her corporate career to pursue education full-time. Alongside Aman Dhattarwal, she co-founded Apna College, offering affordable courses in programming, data structures, web development, and interview preparation. Her simple yet insightful teaching style and mentorship have helped millions of students from tier-2 and tier-3 cities build technical careers, making the channel one of India’s most popular YouTube-based learning communities.


Recognition and influence

Khapra’s approach to democratizing tech education has earned her a loyal following and the nickname “Shradha Didi.” Her story of leaving a ₹43-lakh Microsoft package to teach is frequently cited as an example of purpose-driven career choice. She and Dhattarwal married in March 2023 after a public proposal at Lovely Professional University, a moment that symbolized their shared mission to inspire India’s youth through education.


Impact and outlook

Through Apna College and public initiatives, Shradha Khapra advocates for women in STEM and aims to expand global access to tech skills. Her work continues to bridge the gap between academic learning and industry needs, establishing her as a leading voice in India’s ed-tech renaissance.
